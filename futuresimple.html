Will vs Be Going To ü§î

WILL

Spontaneous decisions:
"I'll help you with that!"
Predictions:
"It will rain tomorrow."
Promises:
"I will call you later."

BE GOING TO

Planned actions:
"I'm going to study tonight."
Predictions with evidence:
"Look at those clouds! It's going to rain."
Intentions:
"We're going to buy a new car."

Grammar Tips! üí°

üîÆ

Use WILL for

Quick decisions, predictions, promises, and offers you make in the moment.

‚ö°

Contractions

Use I'll, you'll, he'll, she'll, we'll, they'll in informal speech.

‚ùå

Won't = Will Not

"Won't" is the contraction for "will not" - don't forget the apostrophe!

üéØ

Base Form Only

Always use the base form of the verb after "will" - never add -s, -ed, or -ing.
