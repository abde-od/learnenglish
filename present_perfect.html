Master the present perfect tense and learn to connect past actions with the present!

How to Use Present Perfect 🔗

✅ Positive Form

have/has + past participle
I have worked here for 5 years.
She has visited Paris.
They have finished homework.

❌ Negative Form

have/has + not + past participle
I haven't worked today.
She hasn't visited Paris.
They haven't finished.

❓ Question Form

Have/Has + subject + past participle?
Have you worked here long?
Has she visited Paris?

🎯 When to Use

• Life experiences
• Unfinished time periods
• Recent past with present result
• Actions continuing to present

Grammar Tips! 💡

🔗

Past ↔ Present Connection

Present perfect connects past actions to the present moment or result.

🎯

Life Experiences

Perfect for talking about experiences: "I have visited Japan."

⏰

Unfinished Time

Use with "today", "this week", "this year" - time periods not finished.

📚

Regular vs Irregular

Regular verbs: work→worked. Irregular: go→gone, see→seen, eat→eaten.
