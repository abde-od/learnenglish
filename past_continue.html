Master the past continuous tense and learn to describe actions that were in progress in the past!

How to Use Past Continuous ‚è™

‚úÖ Positive Form

was/were + verb + ing

She was reading a book.

‚ùå Negative Form

was/were + not + verb + ing

She wasn't reading.
They weren't playing.

‚ùì Question Form

Was/Were + subject + verb + ing?

üéØ When to Use

‚Ä¢ Actions in progress in the past
‚Ä¢ Background actions
‚Ä¢ Interrupted actions

Past Continuous vs Past Simple ü§î

PAST CONTINUOUS

Ongoing action:
"I was studying when you called."
Background action:
"It was raining all day."
Parallel actions:
"While I was cooking, she was cleaning."

PAST SIMPLE

Completed action:
"You called me at 8 PM."
Finished event:
"It rained yesterday."
Sequential actions:
"I cooked dinner, then I cleaned."

Story with Past Continuous üìñ

Yesterday Evening

At 7 PM yesterday, my family was having dinner.

Dad was telling us about his day at work.

Mom was serving the delicious pasta.

My sister was laughing at Dad's funny story.

I was enjoying the time with my family.

While we were eating, it started to rain.

Interrupted Action

I was studying for my English test.

The room was very quiet.

I was reading my textbook carefully.

Suddenly, the phone rang.

It was my best friend calling.

We talked while I was taking a break.

Common Past Continuous Patterns üîß

When + Past Simple

I was sleeping when the alarm rang.
She was cooking when he arrived.
They were playing when it started raining.
Past continuous = background action
Past simple = interrupting action

While + Past Continuous

While I was studying, my brother was watching TV.
While it was raining, we were staying inside.
While she was reading, he was cooking.
Both actions happening at the same time

Grammar Tips! üí°

üé¨

Background Actions

Past continuous sets the scene - like background music in a movie!

‚è±Ô∏è

Duration

Shows actions that continued for some time in the past, not quick actions.

üë•

Was vs Were

Use "was" with I/he/she/it and "were" with you/we/they.

üö´

Avoid with Stative Verbs

Don't use with know, like, want, need - use past simple instead.
